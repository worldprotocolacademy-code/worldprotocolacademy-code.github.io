# WPA Analytics and Metrics Methodology v0.1

**World Protocol Academy**  
**Institute for Protocol, Diplomacy, Public Communication & Security Studies**

## Status

Draft methodology for the WPA Analytics Centre. This is not an accreditation framework, not a professional licence, and not a replacement for official ranking, scholarly indexing or accreditation bodies.

## Core idea

WPA develops a specialized benchmark for fields that are often not measured together: protocol, diplomacy, public communication, security studies, communication skills and professional practice.

## Methodological principles

1. Measurable indicators.
2. Publicly verifiable evidence.
3. Category-based comparison.
4. Separation of academic and professional-practice criteria.
5. Annual review.
6. Correction window for institutions.
7. Conflict-of-interest disclosure.
8. No payment for better ranking.
9. Human review for final publication.
10. No overclaiming.

## Institution groups

- A — Specialized Protocol / Etiquette / Diplomatic Protocol Academies
- B — Diplomatic Academies and Government Training Institutes
- C — Universities / Schools of Diplomacy, International Relations, Law and Political Sciences
- D — Foreign Service Institutes
- E — Professional Protocol & Diplomacy Training Providers
- F — Related Governance / International Education Institutions
- G — Public Communication, PR & Communication Studies
- H — Security, Defence & Strategic Studies
- I — Global Prestige Benchmark Institutions
- R — Official Reference Sources
- WPA — WPA self-entry, requiring independent review if scored

## Draft indicator families

- Research output
- Scholarly visibility
- Faculty / expert authority
- Professional practice authority
- Annual conferences, applied research and exercises
- Programme and curriculum quality
- International engagement
- Public communication and digital transparency
- Ethics, privacy and governance discipline

## Required disclaimer

The WPA Index is a specialized analytical benchmark. It is not state accreditation, not university status, not a professional licence, and not a substitute for Scopus, Web of Science, ORCID, Google Scholar or official accreditation bodies.
