Total volume: more than 440 pages across nine working papers covering dress code, contingency protocol, the 2026 Beijing Summit, comparative Trump and Putin visits to the PRC, the empirical protocol analysis of President Aleksandar Vucic's May 2026 state visit to the PRC, and the state visit of King Charles III to the United States of America, the EU-Western Balkans Summit in Tivat, and the Pyongyang reception connected to Xi Jinping's visit to the DPRK as case studies in ceremonial diplomacy, summit protocol, enlargement protocol, and visual statecraft.

Reading Note · The Chinese Protocol Trilogy (WPA 003 → 004 → 005)

WPA 003, 004, 005, and 007 form a coherent corpus of protocol analyses of state visits to the People’s Republic of China. WPA 005 introduces the WPA Protocol Soft Power Index and WPA 007 further applies PSPI to the Beijing Convergence of Western leaders’ visits to the PRC as a measurable and transferable instrument across the series.

Recommended reading order: WPA 003 → WPA 004 → WPA 005 → WPA 007.

Reading Note · Ceremonial Diplomacy and Soft Power (WPA 006)

WPA 006 expands the WPA Working Paper series beyond the China-focused protocol case studies and introduces a transatlantic royal-state visit case study. It examines how ceremonial diplomacy, royal symbolism, presidential protocol, military honours, gift exchange, commemorative rites, and media framing operate as instruments of soft power and sovereign communication.

Recommended reading connection: WPA 001 → WPA 002 → WPA 006 for readers interested in ceremonial standards, contingency logic, and high-level state-visit protocol.

Reading Note · Enlargement Protocol and Regional Security (WPA 008)

WPA 008 extends the WPA series into EU-Western Balkans summit protocol, candidate-country hosting, enlargement diplomacy, gradual integration and regional security. It introduces the concept of protocol geometry of enlargement as a framework for interpreting the spatial, ceremonial and symbolic architecture of accession-oriented summits.

Recommended reading connection: WPA 002 → WPA 006 → WPA 008 for readers interested in contingency logic, high-level ceremonial diplomacy and multilateral summit protocol.

Reading Note · Visual Statecraft and DPRK / PRC Protocol (WPA 009)

WPA 009 extends the WPA Working Paper series into DPRK / PRC protocol analysis, focusing on reception architecture, ceremonial sequencing, visual statecraft, symbolic hospitality and public communication surrounding the Pyongyang reception connected to Xi Jinping's visit to the DPRK.

Recommended reading connection: WPA 003 → WPA 004 → WPA 005 → WPA 007 → WPA 009 for readers interested in China / PRC protocol, symbolic sequencing and visual diplomatic communication.
