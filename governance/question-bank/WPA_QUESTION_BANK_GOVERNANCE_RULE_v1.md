Director's Adoption Note
This rule is adopted as an internal operational policy of World Protocol Academy. It governs how the Academy organizes, classifies, stores, distributes, and protects its question bank, practice materials, answer keys, and certification examination pools.
This document is a working policy for the discipline of internal operations. It is not a marketing claim, not a public certificate of compliance, and not a representation of certification standing beyond what the Academy's operational practice actually achieves on a given date. Where the Academy's actual practice falls short of this rule, the gap shall be addressed through documented internal work rather than concealed by aspirational language.

Preamble
The integrity of any educational and certification body rests on the discipline with which it manages assessment materials. Items used in a certification examination must remain unseen by candidates until the examination itself; answer keys must not circulate beyond authorized personnel; explanations and source references constitute institutional intellectual property and must be protected accordingly.
This Governance Rule establishes the framework under which World Protocol Academy organizes its question bank, practice materials, answer keys, and certification examination pools. It binds all WPA staff, contractors, advisory board members, and authorized partners who handle Academy assessment materials in any form.
The rule is informed by, but does not assert formal compliance with, established testing standards (AERA/APA/NCME Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing, ITC Guidelines on Test Security, ISO/IEC 17024 for personnel certification). Where the Academy aspires to formal alignment with one or more such standards, that aspiration shall be declared explicitly and pursued through documented work.

Section 1. Scope and Definitions
1.1 Scope
This rule governs all assessment-related content produced, held, or distributed by the Academy, regardless of medium (digital, print, audio-visual) or storage location.
1.2 Status of the existing 2210-question bank
The existing Academy question bank, comprising approximately 2210 questions across batches B1–B8, is classified as Practice / Knowledge Bank under this rule. It is treated as Tier S material (see Section 2). It is not classified as a certification examination pool and shall not be used as such unless and until specific items are formally reviewed, approved, and migrated under the procedures of this rule into a separately constituted certification pool. By default, the existing 2210 bank remains a teaching and self-study resource, not a testing instrument.
1.3 Definitions
Question — A single assessment item consisting of stem, options, designated correct answer, explanation, and source references, conforming to the Academy's frozen 19-field schema.
Question Bank — The complete collection of Academy-produced questions across all batches, regardless of classification tier.
Practice / Knowledge Bank — The Academy's principal teaching and self-study resource, used to support enrolled candidates' preparation. The existing 2210-question bank constitutes the current Practice / Knowledge Bank.
Certification Examination Pool — A separately maintained collection of questions designed exclusively for use in formal Academy certification examinations. Items in this pool are never published, never shared with candidates outside the examination itself, and never overlap with the Practice / Knowledge Bank.
Answer Key — Any record that pairs question identifiers with their correct answers in any form that permits the correct answer to a specific question to be determined.
Full Explanation File — Any record pairing question identifiers with explanations and source references in a form that supports answer-key reconstruction.
Custodian — The WPA staff member designated by the Director as the operational owner of the question bank, accountable for inventory, access logs, and routine compliance with this rule.
Authorized Personnel — Persons explicitly granted access by the Director, in writing, with role-based permissions consistent with this rule.
Material Incident — Any event in which protected material is, or may be, exposed beyond its authorized audience.

Section 2. Classification Tiers
The Academy operates a four-tier classification system. Every item, file, and record produced or held by the Academy shall be assigned to exactly one tier. Reclassification requires written authorization by the Director.
2.1 Tier P — Public
Material intended for public visibility on the Academy website, in marketing communications, and in external publications.
Permitted content: sample question stems without correct-answer indication, illustrative examples used in promotional material, abstract descriptions of the question bank's scope and structure, statistical summaries that do not enable item reconstruction, methodology disclosures, item formats described at conceptual level.
Prohibited content: any content that reveals correct answers, distractor structure of specific items, or sufficient detail to reconstruct individual questions; any content drawn from the certification examination pool.
2.2 Tier S — Student
Material accessible to enrolled candidates and registered users for self-study and practice purposes. The existing 2210-question Practice / Knowledge Bank is Tier S material.
Permitted content: practice questions with stems, options, correct answers, and explanations delivered after candidate engagement; cumulative practice tests assembled from the practice pool; performance feedback to individual candidates; study guides referencing practice questions.
Access mechanism: authenticated access through Academy-controlled platform; no public web indexing; no anonymous access; access tied to verified enrollment record.
Prohibited content: any item from the certification examination pool; staff-only annotations; pre-publication drafts.
2.3 Tier I — Internal
Material accessible to Academy staff and contracted reviewers in the course of academic and operational duties.
Permitted content: complete answer keys for the Practice / Knowledge Bank; complete explanation and source-reference files for the Practice / Knowledge Bank; draft items in development; item review workflows; methodology documentation; audit logs; performance analytics; item-level statistics derived from candidate testing.
Access mechanism: authenticated access on internal systems; role-based permissions; audit logging of access events.
Prohibited content: material from the certification examination pool, unless the staff member's role specifically requires it.
2.4 Tier C — Certification
Material constituting or supporting the certification examination pool. The most strictly controlled category.
Permitted content: items designated for certification use; certification answer keys; certification explanation files; certification psychometric data; examination forms (assemblies of items into specific exam instances); candidate response data for certification examinations; identifying information of certification candidates.
Access mechanism: strict access control based on documented "need to access" for specific certification work; access events individually logged; access list reviewed quarterly by Director.
Default rule: A staff member's general WPA role does not entitle them to Tier C access. Tier C access is granted item-by-item or function-by-function for specific certification cycles.

Section 3. Public Practice Question Rules
3.1 What may be published publicly
The Academy may publish on its public website and in public communications:

A specimen of practice questions, defined as no more than 1% of the total practice pool, selected and rotated quarterly.
Aggregate statistics about the practice pool (total items, topic distribution, batch structure) without item-level detail.
Methodology disclosures describing how items are produced, reviewed, and validated.
The schema and field definitions of the question format.

3.2 Public specimen requirements
Each item published as a public specimen must satisfy all of the following:

Drawn exclusively from Tier S, never from Tier C.
Carries a visible "Specimen" marker in any rendering.
Remains published for no longer than three months before rotation.
Permanently retired from any future certification examination pool if it has ever been published as a specimen.

3.3 What is prohibited in public publication
The following must never appear in any public-facing publication:

Any item from Tier C.
Any answer key, in any form, including partial keys.
Full explanations or source references that enable item reconstruction.
Item-level psychometric statistics that could assist candidates in identifying high-yield items.
File listings, manifests, or directory indexes that disclose the structure or volume of restricted material.
API endpoints or storage URLs from which restricted material can be retrieved without authentication.

3.4 Search engine treatment
Pages presenting Tier P material may permit indexing by search engines under standard SEO practices. All other tiers shall be excluded from indexing through:

HTTP headers (X-Robots-Tag: noindex, nofollow, noarchive at the edge layer).
HTML meta tags where applicable.
Exclusion from the public sitemap.
Authentication preventing unauthenticated retrieval before any indexing decision is made.

robots.txt directives are supplementary, not primary, protection. Reliance on robots.txt alone is insufficient and prohibited.

Section 4. Private Certification Exam Pool Rules
4.1 Construction principle
The certification examination pool shall be built separately from the Practice / Knowledge Bank, by authors who have signed a written confidentiality undertaking. Items in the pool are designed from inception for certification use. Migration of items from Tier S to Tier C is prohibited; only fresh items may enter Tier C.
This is a foundational principle. Any item visible to candidates as practice material is, for examination purposes, a compromised item from the moment of first publication.
4.2 Pool size and composition
The certification pool shall contain a sufficient quantity of validated items to support multiple non-overlapping examination forms within a calendar year, with reserve capacity for emergency form replacement. Minimum pool size for the first operational year shall be established by the Director in consultation with the Academic Advisory Board, informed by the certification's intended scope and stakes.
The pool composition (topic coverage, difficulty distribution, item formats) shall be documented in a Certification Blueprint, approved by the Director and reviewed by the Academic Advisory Board, before any examination is administered.
4.3 Item lifecycle
Each Tier C item passes through documented stages:

Draft — Item written by authorized author.
Review — Item reviewed by at least one second authorized reviewer.
Pilot — Item administered in low-stakes pilot conditions for initial statistics.
Live — Item available for inclusion in operational examination forms.
Retired — Item removed from operational use due to exposure, poor psychometric performance, or maximum permitted use.

A retired item that has been exposed shall not return to live status under any circumstances.
4.4 Examination forms
A specific examination, as administered to a specific cohort on a specific date, is an "examination form" assembled from the live pool. Forms shall be:

Assembled by authorized personnel only, with the assembly record retained as part of the examination archive.
Used for the cohort and date specified, then retired as a form (individual items may continue in the pool subject to exposure assessment).
Never reused as identical forms between cohorts.

4.5 Pool replenishment
The pool shall be replenished continuously. New items enter through the lifecycle in 4.3. The Custodian shall report quarterly to the Director on pool size, item lifecycle status, and replenishment progress.
4.6 No public preview
Under no circumstances shall the certification pool, in whole or in part, be made available for public preview, marketing demonstrations, sample examinations, or any use beyond actual certification administration.
4.7 No cross-contamination with Practice / Knowledge Bank
The Practice / Knowledge Bank (existing 2210 questions) and the certification pool are operationally and physically separate. They reside in different storage buckets (Section 7), use different identifier prefixes (Section 8), and are accessed under different authority (Section 10). Any procedural change that would weaken this separation requires Director approval and Academic Advisory Board consultation.

Section 5. Answer Key Protection Rules
5.1 Definition revisited
An "answer key" is any record permitting the correct answer to a specific identified question to be determined. The definition is broad by design and includes:

Files explicitly labeled as answer keys.
Files containing the correct_answer field alongside question identifiers.
Files containing full questions with the correct answer indicated by any means.
Spreadsheets, databases, or notes that permit answer reconstruction.
Communications revealing correct answers paired with identifiers.

5.2 Storage requirements
Answer keys at any tier shall be stored only in:

Academy authenticated cloud storage with private access (Tier I or Tier C bucket, as appropriate).
Encrypted local archives held by authorized personnel.

Answer keys shall never be stored on:

Public cloud buckets or unauthenticated web-accessible storage.
Personal email accounts or messaging applications.
Unencrypted portable storage.
Any system shared with unauthorized parties.

5.3 Transmission requirements
Answer keys shall be transmitted only via:

Academy authenticated systems with end-to-end encryption.
Approved secure file-sharing platforms with named recipients and finite expiration.
In-person handover of encrypted media for exceptional cases.

Answer keys shall never be transmitted via:

Unencrypted email.
Public messaging applications.
Attachments visible to non-authorized parties.

5.4 Practice pool answer keys (Tier I)
Practice pool answer keys may be released to candidates only after the candidate has completed the corresponding practice item, through the authenticated platform that records the candidate's response first. Bulk release of practice answer keys to candidates is prohibited.
5.5 Certification pool answer keys (Tier C)
Certification answer keys are accessed only by personnel responsible for scoring a specific examination form, only for the duration of the scoring activity, with access revoked upon completion. The list of persons who have accessed each certification answer key shall be retained as a permanent record.
5.6 Disposal of working copies
Working copies of answer keys held outside the master Tier I or Tier C storage shall be destroyed upon completion of the work that authorized their creation. Destruction shall be documented.

Section 6. Explanation and Source-Reference Protection Rules
6.1 Status of explanations
Explanations and source references constitute the Academy's institutional intellectual property. They represent expert work and significant value beyond the questions themselves. Their protection is treated with the same seriousness as answer keys.
6.2 Practice pool explanations
Practice pool explanations may be released to candidates only after the candidate has engaged with the corresponding question. Bulk release of practice explanations as a standalone resource is prohibited. Aggregation into authorized study materials is permitted only by Tier I personnel; such study materials inherit Tier S access restrictions.
6.3 Certification pool explanations
Certification pool explanations follow the same access control as certification items themselves. They are not released to candidates after the examination, except in summary form within the examination feedback report and only at the level required by the Academy's published feedback policy.
6.4 Source references
Source references identify the authoritative material on which an item is based. They are protected at the same tier as the item itself. The Academy's published methodology may name categories of sources used and representative authoritative works in the field, but specific source-to-item mappings remain protected.

Each non-public bucket shall be backed up at least weekly to a separate location with equivalent or stronger access controls. Backup retention shall follow the audit retention requirements of Section 11.

Section 8. Naming Convention for Files
8.1 Standard file name format
All question bank files shall use the following naming pattern:
WPA-QB-{TIER}-{POOL}-{BATCH}-{RANGE}-{TYPE}-v{VERSION}.{ext}
Where:

TIER — one of PUB, STU, INT, CERT
POOL — Practice, Certification, or Specimen
BATCH — batch identifier (e.g., B1, B5, B8)
RANGE — question ID range (e.g., Q001-Q250, CertQ0001-CertQ0200)
TYPE — one of Items, Key, Full, Manifest, Statistics
VERSION — version number per Section 9
ext — file extension (json, csv, pdf, docx)

8.2 Examples
WPA-QB-STU-Practice-B5-Q1001-Q1250-Items-v1.0.json
WPA-QB-INT-Practice-B5-Q1001-Q1250-Key-v1.0.csv
WPA-QB-INT-Practice-B5-Q1001-Q1250-Full-v1.0.json
WPA-QB-CERT-Certification-C1-CertQ0001-CertQ0200-Items-v1.0.json
WPA-QB-CERT-Certification-C1-CertQ0001-CertQ0200-Key-v1.0.csv
WPA-QB-PUB-Specimen-S2026Q1-Items-v1.0.json
8.3 Identifier separation between practice and certification
To minimize the risk of cross-tier confusion, the certification pool shall use identifier prefix CertQ (rather than Q), with its own numeric sequence starting at CertQ0001. This makes any accidental commingling of practice and certification identifiers immediately visible.
8.4 Prohibited naming practices

Filenames not declaring the tier (e.g., WPA-QB-Items.json).
Filenames using ambiguous abbreviations not defined in this rule.
Files that mix tiers in a single payload (e.g., a file containing both Tier S and Tier C items) — such files are prohibited entirely.


Section 9. Versioning Convention
9.1 Semantic versioning
Files shall use semantic versioning in the form vMAJOR.MINOR.PATCH:

MAJOR — Substantial content changes: items added or removed in volume, structural schema changes, batch reorganization.
MINOR — Meaningful but compatible changes: shuffle pass applied, distractors strengthened, language cleanup pass.
PATCH — Small corrections: typo fixes, single-item corrections, metadata updates.

9.2 Version history
Each batch shall maintain a version history record (WPA-QB-{TIER}-{POOL}-{BATCH}-VERSIONS.md) listing every version produced, with date, author, change summary, and approval reference.
9.3 Active version
At any time, exactly one version of a batch is the "active" version for operational use. Earlier versions are archived but not active. The active version is identified in the master manifest.
9.4 Version retention
All versions are retained per the audit retention requirements of Section 11. Versions are not deleted; they are marked archived.

Access shall be revoked promptly when the role that justified access ends, the contracted work is completed, the confidentiality undertaking is breached, or the Director determines that revocation is required. 10.6 Audit
The Custodian shall maintain a current list of access grants with role, scope, duration, and authorization date. The list shall be reviewed by the Director quarterly.

Section 12. Emergency Response if Exam Material Becomes Public
12.1 Definition of an exposure event
An exposure event is any circumstance in which material classified as Tier S, I, or C is, or may be, accessible to persons outside its authorized audience. Suspected exposure shall be treated as an exposure event until verified otherwise.
12.2 Reporting
Any person who becomes aware of a possible exposure event shall report it without delay to the Custodian or, if the Custodian is unavailable or implicated, directly to the Director. Reports shall not be filtered, delayed, or normalized.
12.3 Initial response (first 24 hours)
Upon notification, within 24 hours:

The Custodian or Director verifies the exposure (whether real, scope, channel, duration).
The exposed material is removed or made inaccessible from the channel where the Academy has control.
Where the channel is outside Academy control, takedown procedures are initiated.
Access tokens potentially involved are revoked and reissued.
Initial scope assessment is documented.

12.4 Material incident report
Within seven calendar days of verification, the Custodian shall produce a Material Incident Report containing:

Description of the exposure event.
Identification of the material exposed.
Scope of exposure (channel, duration, estimated reach).
Root cause analysis.
Immediate response actions taken.
Remediation plan with assigned responsibility and deadlines.
Lessons identified.
Recommendations for governance changes if applicable.

The report is reviewed and approved by the Director before being filed in the permanent record.
12.5 Examination integrity decisions
Where exposure affects Tier C material, the Director shall determine within 14 days whether:

Affected items are retired permanently from the certification pool.
Affected examination forms are withdrawn from current or planned use.
Recently administered examinations are subject to score review or invalidation.
Affected candidates are offered remedy (re-examination, extended preparation period, or other appropriate action).

The presumption is in favor of stronger remedy: when in doubt, the Director shall err toward retiring affected material, withdrawing affected forms, and providing affected candidates with the option of re-examination at no additional cost.
12.6 External communication
Where exposure has consequences for candidates or external parties, the Director determines appropriate communication, in consultation with legal counsel where appropriate. Communication shall be honest about the event, its scope, and the Academy's response.
The Academy shall not minimize, misrepresent, or conceal exposure events. Honest communication, even where uncomfortable, builds the credibility on which the Academy's standing depends.
12.7 Notification to affected persons
Where personal data of identifiable persons is exposed, the Academy shall comply with applicable data protection notification requirements, including notification to the Personal Data Protection Agency of the Republic of North Macedonia and to affected data subjects where required by law.
12.8 Post-incident review
Within 30 days of resolution, the Director shall convene a post-incident review with the Custodian and other relevant personnel. The review examines:

Whether existing controls were adequate.
Whether existing controls were followed.
What governance changes, if any, are warranted.
Whether any personnel action is required.

The post-incident review record is retained per Section 11.2.
12.9 Advisory Board notification
The Director shall notify the Academic Advisory Board of any material incident at the next scheduled meeting, or earlier if the incident is of sufficient gravity. The Advisory Board shall have access to the Material Incident Report and post-incident review record on request.

Section 13. Roles and Responsibilities
13.1 Director
The Director is ultimately accountable for question bank governance and:

Approves this rule and any subsequent amendments.
Designates the Custodian.
Authorizes Tier I and Tier C access grants.
Reviews quarterly governance reports from the Custodian.
Convenes annual audits.
Decides examination integrity questions following exposure events.
Decides external communication regarding incidents.

13.2 Question Bank Custodian
The Custodian is operationally responsible for day-to-day governance compliance and:

Maintains the master inventory and manifest of all question bank files.
Maintains access grant records.
Conducts monthly verification of public bucket compliance.
Produces quarterly governance reports.
Receives and verifies exposure event reports.
Produces Material Incident Reports.
Coordinates routine audit activities.

13.3 Item authors and reviewers
Item authors and reviewers are responsible for:

Compliance with their confidentiality undertaking.
Use of authorized systems and channels for handling assessment material.
Reporting any suspected exposure event without delay.
Returning or destroying working copies upon completion of contracted work.

13.4 Certification staff
Certification staff are responsible for:

Strict adherence to Tier C handling requirements during examination preparation, administration, and scoring.
Maintenance of examination form integrity.
Documentation of all access events involving Tier C material during their work.
Reporting any suspected exposure event without delay.

13.5 Academy general staff
All Academy staff, regardless of specific role, are responsible for:

Awareness of this rule and the classification system.
Reporting any suspected exposure event without delay.
Refraining from any handling of Tier I or Tier C material outside their authorized scope.

13.6 Academic Advisory Board
The Academic Advisory Board's responsibilities relevant to this rule:

Review and provide guidance on the rule itself, both at adoption and at review cycles.
Receive notifications of material incidents.
Conduct or commission independent reviews when requested by the Director.
Recommend governance improvements.


Section 14. Phased Implementation
This rule shall be implemented through four sequential phases. Each phase has a defined objective and a definition of "done." The Director may proceed to the next phase only when the previous phase is complete and documented.
14.1 Phase 1 — Secure the Legacy Bucket (week 1)
Objective. Treat the existing protocol-kb bucket as a legacy secured bucket. Confirm and maintain the disabled state of public access. Conduct an inventory of its contents.
Actions.

Confirm protocol-kb public access remains disabled.
Verify by external test (unauthenticated request) that the bucket and its objects are not retrievable from the public internet.
Produce a complete inventory of files currently in protocol-kb, recording for each file: name, size, date, intended classification tier under this rule.
Restrict access to protocol-kb to the Director and the designated Custodian only, pending migration in Phase 4.

Definition of done. External test confirms bucket is not publicly accessible. Inventory document exists and is signed off by the Custodian.
14.2 Phase 2 — Establish the Public Bucket (weeks 2–3)
Objective. Provision a new public bucket dedicated to Tier P material only, with no commingling of restricted material.
Actions.

Create new bucket wpa-public with public read access enabled.
Define and document what may reside in this bucket (Tier P only, per Section 2.1 and Section 3).
Migrate or upload only materials that have been classified as Tier P during Phase 1 inventory.
Verify that no file from protocol-kb has been transferred to wpa-public without explicit reclassification approval.
Configure search engine indexing controls on Academy public website pages per Section 3.4.

Definition of done. wpa-public exists, contains only Tier P material, and the Custodian has documented a verification check confirming no restricted material has been moved into it.
14.3 Phase 3 — Establish the Certification Bucket (weeks 4–5)
Objective. Provision a new private bucket dedicated to Tier C material, with strict access controls, in preparation for any future certification pool.
Actions.

Create new bucket wpa-certification with public access disabled and additional restrictions per Section 7.2.
Define access controls: pre-signed URLs only, time-limited tokens, named-recipient access.
Document the access control configuration.
Confirm that no Tier C material is created or migrated into this bucket until the certification pool is constructed under the discipline of this rule. The bucket may exist empty until certification work begins.
Confirm that no item from the existing protocol-kb bucket or the future wpa-student bucket is migrated into wpa-certification. The certification pool, when built, shall be built from scratch.

Definition of done. wpa-certification exists with documented access controls. Custodian has confirmed in writing that no cross-tier migration into this bucket has occurred.
14.4 Phase 4 — Migrate and Rename Files (weeks 6–10)
Objective. Migrate the legacy protocol-kb contents into the appropriate new buckets (wpa-student, wpa-internal) and apply the naming convention of Section 8.
Actions.

Provision wpa-student and wpa-internal buckets per Section 7.1.
Based on Phase 1 inventory and tier assignments, migrate each file from protocol-kb to the appropriate destination bucket.
Rename files to comply with the naming convention of Section 8.
Update internal manifests, references, and documentation to point to new file locations and names.
After verification that all files have been migrated and renamed, decommission the protocol-kb bucket or retain it as a frozen archive with no further write activity.
Document the migration with a final inventory mapping legacy file names to new file names.

Definition of done. All files have been migrated and renamed. The migration mapping document exists and is signed off by the Director. protocol-kb is either decommissioned or frozen as a read-only archive.
14.5 Confirmation of foundational separations
At the end of Phase 4, the Custodian shall confirm in writing to the Director that:

The existing 2210-question bank is held in wpa-student (Tier S) and wpa-internal (Tier I, for keys and explanations), classified as Practice / Knowledge Bank.
No content from the Practice / Knowledge Bank has been migrated into wpa-certification.
Any future certification pool will be constructed separately, under Section 4 of this rule.


Section 15. Amendment and Review
15.1 Annual review
This rule shall be reviewed annually by the Director, in consultation with the Custodian and the Academic Advisory Board. The review shall consider operational experience, incident history, evolving best practices, and evolving legal requirements. Material amendments require Director approval and are communicated to all personnel with question bank responsibilities.
15.2 Ad hoc review
Any material incident shall trigger ad hoc review of relevant rule sections within 30 days of incident resolution.
15.3 Version control of this rule
This rule itself is versioned per Section 9. Earlier versions are archived. The active version is identified in the document header.

Section 16. Limitations and Honest Framing
16.1 Internal operational policy, not public marketing
This rule is an internal operational policy of World Protocol Academy. It does not constitute a public marketing claim, a certificate of compliance with any external standard, or a representation that the Academy currently meets requirements that have not yet been operationally achieved.
The Academy shall not, in any external communication, represent adoption of this rule as equivalent to compliance with formal testing or certification standards. The Academy may state, accurately, that it has adopted an internal governance rule; the Academy may not state, inaccurately, that it operates a certification regime at the level implied by this rule's strictest provisions before such operation has actually been built.
16.2 Aspiration toward formal standards
The rule is designed to be compatible with movement toward formal alignment with established testing standards over time. Where the Academy pursues such alignment, progress shall be reported in measurable terms in the Academy's Annual Report rather than asserted as already achieved.
16.3 Honest gap acknowledgment
Where the Academy's actual governance practice falls short of this rule, the gap shall be acknowledged internally and addressed through documented work. The rule is an internal commitment, and the Academy's credibility depends on visible adherence over time, not on the rule's existence on paper.

Annex B. Quick Reference — What May and May Not Be Public
May be public (Tier P):

Sample question stems without correct-answer indication, marked "Specimen."
Aggregate statistics about the practice pool.
Methodology disclosures.
Schema and field definitions.
Marketing material describing the question bank conceptually.

May never be public:

Any item from the certification pool, in any form.
Any answer key, full or partial.
Full explanations or source-to-item mappings.
Item-level psychometric statistics.
File listings or directory indexes that disclose restricted material structure.
Storage URLs from which restricted material can be retrieved without authentication.
