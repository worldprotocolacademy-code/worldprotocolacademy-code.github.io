## Director's Adoption Note

This document establishes the Terms of Reference for the World Protocol Academy's Academic Advisory Board. It is adopted as an internal governance document defining an advisory body that supports the Academy's research discipline, ethics, privacy, publication quality, and institutional realism.

This document does not constitute, and shall not be represented as constituting, formal academic accreditation, university status, mutual recognition with any other institution, or any external institutional standing the Academy has not actually earned. The Academic Advisory Board exists to strengthen the Academy's internal discipline; it does not, by its existence, alter the Academy's external recognition status.

## Preamble

The Academy is a specialized institute and a growing international platform for protocol, diplomacy, public communication, and security studies. As the Academy develops its internal governance, research methodology, question bank discipline, privacy practice, and aspirations toward certification integrity, it requires advisory input from persons whose academic, professional, and ethical authority extends beyond the Director and the Academy's immediate staff.

The Academic Advisory Board is established to provide that input. Its role is advisory: to inform, challenge, and improve the Academy's work, not to govern, manage, or direct it. The Director remains the executive authority of the Academy. The Board's contribution is the discipline of independent review by qualified persons whose advice the Director takes seriously and acts upon where appropriate.

This document defines who the Board members are, what they do, what they do not do, how they work, and how their contribution is recorded. The framing is deliberately modest. An advisory board's value is built over time through honest engagement with substantive matters. It is not built by the Board's existence alone, nor by representations about the Board's standing.

## 1. Purpose of the Academic Advisory Board

The Academic Advisory Board (“the Board”) is a standing advisory body of the World Protocol Academy. Its purpose is to:

Strengthen the credibility and discipline of the Academy's research, publications, and educational activities.

Provide independent academic, professional, and ethical perspectives on the Academy's governance documents, methodology, and operational practice.

Advise the Director on intellectual direction, scholarly priorities, and engagement with the international academic community in the WPA fields.

Identify risks, weaknesses, and gaps the Director may not have anticipated.

Support the Academy's institutional realism: ensuring that what the Academy claims is consistent with what the Academy actually does.

The Board's purpose is institution-building, not institution-marketing. Its existence is internal infrastructure, not promotional content.

## 2. Advisory Nature — Not Executive Governance, Not Management Authority

The Board is advisory only. This characterization is fundamental and is preserved throughout this document.

### 2.1 What “advisory” means

The Board offers advice; the Director decides.

The Board reviews; the Director acts on the review or, where reasoned, declines to.

The Board recommends; the Director implements, defers, or sets aside the recommendation, with reasons recorded.

Where the Director does not act on Board advice, this is not insubordination by the Director; it is the proper exercise of executive responsibility, with the Board's view recorded for the institutional record.

### 2.2 What the Board's role is not

The Board does not direct the Academy's operations.

The Board does not appoint or dismiss Academy staff or contractors.

The Board does not approve or block expenditure or contracts.

The Board does not represent the Academy externally.

The Board does not grant accreditation, recognition, or institutional standing.

Individual Board members do not act on behalf of the Academy without explicit written authorization from the Director for a specific purpose.

### 2.3 Why this matters

The advisory characterization protects both the Director and the Board members. The Director retains executive accountability; Board members are not implicated in operational decisions they did not make and may not have endorsed. The Board's independence is preserved precisely because it does not carry executive responsibility for outcomes.

## 3. Composition

### 3.1 Size

The Board has between five and seven members. The number is set by the Director within this range, considering the breadth of expertise needed and the operational manageability of the Board.

### 3.2 Initial composition

The first Board may begin at five members and grow to seven as recruitment permits. Beginning small with the right members is preferred to filling all positions with members of variable suitability.

### 3.3 Stability of composition

Board membership is stable. Members serve fixed terms (Section 15). Mid-term changes are exceptional and follow Section 21.

### 3.4 Chair

The Director may designate a member as Chair of the Board, or the Board may select its own Chair from among its members. The Chair facilitates meetings and signs the annual summary report; the Chair has no executive authority beyond facilitation. Where no Chair is designated, the Director facilitates Board meetings.

## 4. Recommended Member Profiles

The Board's value depends on the qualifications and independence of its members. The following profiles guide recruitment. The Director may recruit members who fit one or more profiles; the goal is collective coverage of the profiles, not strict one-to-one mapping.

| Profile | Status | Indicative qualifications |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Senior academic in a WPA field | Required | Doctoral degree; sustained academic record (publications, teaching, doctoral supervision) in diplomacy, international relations, protocol studies, public communication, or security studies; preferably affiliated with an established university or research institute. |
| Senior protocol or diplomatic practitioner | Required | Substantial career in state protocol service, diplomatic service, or comparable international institution (typically 15+ years); demonstrated experience with operational protocol or diplomatic practice; current or former senior position. |
| Public communication / PR expert | Required | Senior practitioner or academic in public communication, strategic communication, public relations, or media studies; experience working with institutional reputation, public communication strategy, or government/diplomatic communication. |
| Ethics, privacy, or research integrity specialist | Required | Specialist in research ethics, data protection, research integrity, or related field; ideally with experience on institutional ethics committees, academic integrity boards, or data protection authorities. |
| International member outside North Macedonia | Required | Drawn from any of the above profiles; resident or based outside the Republic of North Macedonia; brings external perspective and supports the Academy's international engagement. |
| Certification or assessment methodology specialist | Optional | Specialist in psychometrics, certification design, educational measurement, or comparable field; particularly relevant if and when WPA develops formal certification programs. May be added when certification work begins. |

### 4.1 General qualification expectations

Beyond profile-specific qualifications, all Board members are expected to bring:

Senior standing in their field (typically established career, recognizable record).

Independence — no current employment by, contractual dependence on, or close personal relationship with the Academy or the Director that would compromise judgment.

Willingness to engage substantively with WPA materials, not merely lend a name.

Capacity to dissent constructively when their judgment differs from the Director's.

Acceptance of the limits of the advisory role and commitment to operating within them.

### 4.2 Diversity of perspective

The Board's effectiveness improves when its members bring different perspectives. The Director gives reasonable consideration in recruitment to:

Diversity of disciplinary backgrounds across the WPA fields.

Diversity of professional backgrounds (academic, practitioner, regulatory, civil society).

Geographic diversity.

Gender balance and other demographic considerations consistent with academic and professional norms.

Diversity is pursued as a means to better advice, not as a marketing virtue. Recruitment is on the merits of contribution; the goal is the strongest Board, not the most photogenic one.

## 5. Balance Between Macedonian/Regional and International Members

The Academy is based in the Republic of North Macedonia and serves both Macedonian and international audiences. The Board's composition reflects this dual orientation.

### 5.1 Indicative balance

At least one member should be drawn from outside North Macedonia and the immediate region (Western Balkans). This international member is required (per Section 4) and supports the Academy's international engagement.

Macedonian and regional members bring familiarity with the Academy's primary operating context, the Macedonian academic and protocol environment, and the regional diplomatic and security context.

A reasonable composition is two to three Macedonian/regional members, two to three international members, balanced as recruitment permits.

### 5.2 Working language

Board working language is English. Macedonian-language materials may be made available with English summaries; complete reliance on Macedonian-only materials would unduly burden international members and is avoided. Where a specific item requires Macedonian-language expertise, the relevant Macedonian/regional members may handle it as a sub-meeting (Section 16).

## 6. Duties of the Board

The Board's substantive contribution is delivered through a defined set of duties. The duties below are aggregate; individual members contribute per their expertise and the matters under consideration.

| Duty area | Specific contributions |
| --- | --- |
| Research methodology guidance | Review and advise on the WPA Research Methodology (WPA-METH-001); recommend revisions; identify gaps; suggest emerging best practice from international scholarly community. |
| Question bank governance review | Periodically review the WPA Question Bank Governance Rule (WPA-GOV-QB-001); receive Custodian's annual report; advise on certification pool construction when initiated. |
| Privacy and AI-use discipline review | Review the Privacy Review Checklist (WPA-PRIV-SD-001) at least annually; advise on AI-assisted workflow disclosure; flag risks the Director may not have anticipated. |
| Publication review | Review monographs, major reports, and benchmark methodologies before publication where the Director requests; provide written feedback; identify methodological weaknesses, factual gaps, and overclaiming. |
| Certification integrity oversight | Where WPA develops formal certification programs, advise on integrity, separation between practice and exam material, and avoidance of overclaiming about the certification's standing. |
| Strategic and intellectual direction advice | Advise the Director on the Academy's intellectual direction, research priorities, conference agendas, and engagement with international scholarly community. |
| Material incident review | Receive notifications of material incidents (per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §12); review post-incident reports; advise on governance improvements arising from incidents. |
| Annual summary report contribution | Contribute to the Board's annual summary report (Section 18) reflecting the Board's view of the Academy's progress and recommended directions. |

### 6.1 Duty execution

Duties are executed at meetings (Section 16), through written feedback on documents circulated by the Director, through ad hoc consultations, and through the Board's annual summary report (Section 18).

### 6.2 Time commitment expectations

Board service involves an indicative time commitment of:

One annual meeting (half-day to one day).

Two quarterly working meetings (60–90 minutes each).

Reading and responding to documents circulated between meetings (typical estimate: 20–40 hours per year).

Ad hoc consultations as needed.

The Director shall not impose additional commitments beyond what members agreed to upon appointment without the member's explicit consent.

## 7. What the Board Does Not Do

Boundary statements protect the Board's role and the Director's authority. The Board does not:

Manage Academy operations or staff.

Approve or veto Academy budgets, contracts, or commercial arrangements.

Hire or fire Academy personnel or contractors.

Represent the Academy externally except where individually authorized in writing for a specific purpose.

Confer accreditation, formal recognition, or institutional standing on the Academy.

Substitute for external peer review (per WPA-METH-001 §8.3, Board review is not peer review and shall not be represented as such).

Substitute for legal counsel; legal questions are referred to qualified legal advisors.

Substitute for regulatory authorities (data protection, accreditation, professional licensing); the Board's review does not satisfy regulatory obligations.

Endorse Academy marketing material; if asked to lend their name to promotional content, members may decline and the Director shall not press.

## 8. Role in Reviewing WPA Research Methodology

The WPA Research Methodology (WPA-METH-001) is the framework against which Academy research is conducted. The Board's review of the methodology is a recurring duty.

### 8.1 Annual methodology review

The Director presents the current version of WPA-METH-001 at the annual meeting.

The Board reviews the document for: alignment with international scholarly practice, internal consistency, completeness, and operational applicability.

The Board recommends revisions; the Director considers and incorporates as appropriate, with reasoning recorded for declined recommendations.

### 8.2 Mid-cycle methodology issues

Where a methodology issue arises between annual meetings (e.g., a specific publication raises a question the methodology does not adequately address), the Director may convene an ad hoc consultation with the relevant Board members.

### 8.3 Methodology version updates

Material amendments to WPA-METH-001 are made by the Director with consultation of the Board. The Board's role is to ensure that amendments are sound, not to gatekeep amendments the Director has decided are necessary.

## 9. Role in Reviewing WPA Question Bank Governance

The WPA Question Bank Governance Rule (WPA-GOV-QB-001) establishes the framework for the Academy's question bank, practice materials, answer keys, and any future certification examination pool. The Board's review of question bank governance has specific dimensions.

### 9.1 Annual governance review

The Custodian's annual report (per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §11) is presented to the Board.

The Board reviews compliance with the four-tier classification, bucket architecture, and access controls.

The Board advises on any gaps between the rule and operational practice.

### 9.2 Material incidents

The Board is notified of any material incident affecting question bank material (per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §12.9).

The Board has access to the Material Incident Report and post-incident review on request.

The Board may recommend governance changes arising from incidents.

### 9.3 Certification pool construction

If and when the Academy initiates construction of a certification examination pool (Tier C under the Governance Rule), the Board's role expands:

The Board reviews the Certification Blueprint (per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §4.2) before any examination is administered.

The Board advises on item lifecycle (Draft → Review → Pilot → Live → Retired), examination form composition, and pool replenishment.

The Board reviews proposed item authors and reviewers for independence and qualification.

Where a certification or assessment methodology specialist is on the Board (per Section 4 optional profile), this member leads the certification-related review with full Board endorsement.

### 9.4 Limits

The Board does not handle Tier C material as a routine matter. Where review of specific items is needed, access is granted item-specific and time-bounded with confidentiality undertaking, per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §10.3.

## 10. Role in Reviewing Student Desk Privacy and AI-Use Discipline

### 10.1 Annual privacy review

The Director presents the current state of the Student Desk and any related Academy tools at the annual meeting.

The Privacy Review Checklist (WPA-PRIV-SD-001) findings from the most recent quarterly review are summarized.

The Board reviews and advises on persistent privacy or data exposure risks, the Academy's response to past incidents, and emerging risks (changes in technology, regulation, threat landscape).

### 10.2 AI-use disclosure

The Board reviews the Academy's AI-assisted workflow disclosures (per WPA-METH-001 §7) for adequacy and accuracy.

The Board advises on emerging norms in AI use across academic and educational institutions; the Academy's discipline is calibrated to evolve with these norms.

Where Board members have specific expertise in AI ethics, data governance, or technology policy, their input on this dimension is given particular weight.

### 10.3 Tools beyond Student Desk

The Board's privacy and AI-use review extends to other Academy tools (WPAWS, Virtual Sande, future tools). The same discipline applies: classification, exposure assessment, disclosure, response procedures.

## 11. Role in Publication Review and Research Quality

### 11.1 Routine publication review

The Board does not review every Academy publication. Routine working papers, policy briefs, and shorter outputs proceed under internal Academy review per WPA-METH-001 §11.3 without Board involvement.

### 11.2 Substantive publication review

The Director may submit substantive publications to the Board for review before publication. This is particularly appropriate for:

Monographs.

Major reports and benchmark methodologies.

Publications addressing sensitive subject matter (current diplomatic events, named institutions, contested historical events).

Publications that establish new doctrinal positions associated with the Academy.

### 11.3 Review process for submitted publications

The Director identifies one or two Board members whose expertise is most relevant to the publication.

The publication is circulated with sufficient time for substantive review (typically four to six weeks).

Reviewing members provide written feedback to the Director.

The Director addresses substantive feedback in revision; where the Director declines to incorporate feedback, the reasoning is communicated to the reviewing member.

Board review is recorded in the publication's internal review history but is not represented to readers as peer review.

### 11.4 What Board review accomplishes and does not accomplish

Board review accomplishes:

Identification of factual errors, methodological weaknesses, and overclaiming.

Constructive critique that improves the work before public release.

Independent attestation, internal to the Academy, that the work has been seen by qualified reviewers.

Board review does not accomplish:

Peer review under the conventions of journals or scholarly publishers (which requires anonymous, structured review by independent specialists external to the institution).

Verification of every factual claim in a substantial work.

Substitution for the author's responsibility for accuracy.

The Academy is careful in describing publications: “internally reviewed, including by Academic Advisory Board members” is accurate where applicable; “peer-reviewed by the Academic Advisory Board” is not.

## 12. Role in Certification Integrity and Avoiding Overclaiming

### 12.1 Certification integrity

Where the Academy operates or develops certification programs, the Board's role in certification integrity includes:

Reviewing the separation between practice materials and certification examination materials (per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §4.7).

Reviewing the design of certification examinations for psychometric soundness, fairness, and security.

Reviewing candidate appeals processes (where applicable).

Reviewing communications with certification holders and prospective candidates for accuracy about what the certification represents.

### 12.2 Avoiding overclaiming

The Board has a specific role in challenging overclaiming. This is one of the duties for which independent review is most valuable, because overclaiming is often invisible to those producing the language.

The Board reviews public-facing communications about the Academy, its certifications, and its programs for accuracy.

The Board flags language that suggests standing the Academy has not earned (“world-class,” “internationally accredited,” “recognized by [body],” etc.).

The Board recommends modest, accurate framings (“specialized institute,” “growing international platform,” “regional reference”).

The Board's recommendation is taken seriously; the Director shall not lightly override Board feedback that public communications contain overclaiming.

### 12.3 Public Annual Report

Where the Academy produces a public Annual Report, the Board reviews it before release for compliance with the no-overclaiming discipline. This review is one of the highest-leverage uses of Board attention.

## 13. Conflict of Interest Policy

### 13.1 Declaration at appointment

Upon appointment, each Board member completes a written conflict of interest declaration covering:

Financial interests in entities engaged with the Academy commercially.

Family relationships with the Director, Academy staff, or other Board members.

Current or prior contractual engagements with the Academy.

Positions on competing or affiliated boards or committees.

Any other circumstance that could create real or perceived conflict.

### 13.2 Ongoing disclosure

Members shall disclose new or emerging conflicts promptly when they arise. Disclosure is not punishment; concealment is what creates institutional risk.

### 13.3 Recusal

Where a Board matter implicates a member's conflict, the member recuses from the discussion and any advice on that matter. Recusal is recorded. The remaining Board members may proceed with the matter; if the recusal leaves insufficient Board capacity, the Director may consult external advisors.

### 13.4 Director's conflicts

The Director's inherent institutional-interest conflict (per WPA-METH-001 §10.3) is acknowledged. The Board's independent review is one of the principal mechanisms by which the Director's institutional-interest conflict is balanced.

### 13.5 Compensation and conflict

Whether Board members receive compensation (honoraria, expense reimbursement, or unpaid service) is set by the Director. Where compensation exists, it is documented and consistent across members. Compensation does not alter the independence requirement; members compensated for their service still owe independent judgment.

## 14. Confidentiality Obligations

### 14.1 General confidentiality

Board members handle confidential Academy material in the course of their duties. Confidentiality applies to:

Tier I and Tier C material under WPA-GOV-QB-001 (only where access is granted item-specific per §10.3 of that rule).

Pre-publication drafts of Academy works.

Material incident details.

Personnel matters.

Strategic discussions about Academy direction.

Identities of external parties (interviewees, candidates, partners) named in materials reviewed by the Board.

### 14.2 Confidentiality undertaking

Each Board member signs a confidentiality undertaking upon appointment, modeled on the WPA Confidentiality Undertaking template (companion document to WPA-GOV-QB-001), tailored to advisory role.

### 14.3 Duration

Confidentiality obligations continue beyond the end of the member's term, for the duration specified in the undertaking (typically 10 years from cessation of service, or as agreed).

### 14.4 Use of Board materials

Board materials are not used by members for purposes other than the Board's work. Members do not cite or reference Board-restricted material in their own external publications, lectures, or commentary without explicit prior written approval from the Director.

### 14.5 Public statements

Members may publicly identify themselves as members of the WPA Academic Advisory Board, subject to Section 23 framing constraints. Members shall not represent positions of the Board or the Academy in public statements unless explicitly authorized.

## 15. Term Length

### 15.1 Standard term

Members serve three-year terms. Terms are renewable once (for a total of six years of continuous service).

### 15.2 Staggered terms

Initial appointments may be made for terms of two, three, or four years to establish a staggered renewal pattern. Staggered terms preserve institutional continuity by avoiding simultaneous turnover of the entire Board.

### 15.3 Beyond second term

After two consecutive terms, a member rotates off the Board for at least one year before being eligible for re-appointment. This avoids indefinite tenure and refreshes Board perspective.

### 15.4 Mid-term changes

Mid-term changes (resignation, removal, vacancy) follow Section 21.

## 16. Meeting Rhythm

Board work proceeds through regular and ad hoc meetings as set out below.

| Meeting type | Frequency | Purpose and format |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Annual full meeting | Once per year | All Board members convene (in person where possible, virtual otherwise) for a full agenda covering annual review of governance documents, research methodology, certification integrity, and the Board's annual summary report. Typically half a day or one day. |
| Quarterly working meeting | Twice per year (between annuals) | Shorter virtual meetings (60–90 minutes) addressing specific items requiring Board attention: review of a specific publication, response to a material incident, progress on a remediation plan, advice on a strategic question. |
| Ad hoc consultation | As needed | Director or individual Board members may convene focused consultations on specific matters between scheduled meetings. Typically by written exchange or short call. Records are retained per Section 17. |
| Subject-matter sub-meetings | As needed | Where a matter falls within the expertise of one or two Board members, the Director may consult those members directly without convening the full Board. The full Board is informed of such consultations at the next scheduled meeting. |

### 16.1 Meeting format

Annual meetings: in person where feasible, with virtual attendance accommodated for international members. Held in Skopje or at a location associated with an Academy event.

Quarterly working meetings: virtual, scheduled at least four weeks in advance.

Ad hoc consultations: by written exchange, short call, or as agreed by participants.

### 16.2 Agenda

The Director circulates an agenda and supporting documents at least 10 working days before scheduled meetings. Members may propose agenda additions until five working days before. Last-minute agenda changes are reserved for genuinely urgent matters.

### 16.3 Attendance

Members are expected to attend all scheduled meetings. Unable to attend, members may submit written input on agenda items to be considered in their absence. Persistent non-attendance may be grounds for removal under Section 21.

## 17. Quorum and Decision/Advice Recording

### 17.1 Quorum

Quorum for an annual or quarterly meeting is the majority of currently appointed members. A meeting without quorum may proceed for discussion but does not produce formal Board advice; advice is finalized by written consent of a quorum subsequent to the meeting.

### 17.2 Mode of advice

The Board operates by consensus where possible. Where consensus is not achievable, the Board records the range of views, identifying any minority position and the reasoning. The Director receives both the majority view and any minority view; the Director's decision considers both.

### 17.3 Recording

Each meeting produces a written record (minutes) capturing: date, attendance, agenda, advice given, decisions of the Director arising from the meeting, action items.

Minutes are circulated to members within 15 working days for confirmation.

Confirmed minutes are retained per the Academy's record retention practice (consistent with WPA-GOV-QB-001 §11).

Where minutes contain confidential matters, restricted versions are produced for circulation outside the Board, with sensitive content redacted.

### 17.4 Voting (where used)

The Board does not vote in the manner of an executive body. Where formal expressions of view are needed, members provide written positions on specific questions, which are recorded individually rather than aggregated into binary outcomes. This preserves the substance of advice over the appearance of decision-making the Board does not have authority to perform.

## 18. Annual Advisory Summary Report

### 18.1 Purpose

Once per year, the Board produces an Annual Advisory Summary Report (“the Annual Report”). The Annual Report is the Board's principal written contribution to the Academy's institutional record and serves as the Board's accountability to the Director and, indirectly, to the broader Academy community.

### 18.2 Content

The Annual Report addresses, in measured language:

The Board's view of the Academy's progress on research methodology, publication quality, governance, privacy, and certification integrity.

Specific recommendations made during the year, with the status of each (acted on, declined, in progress).

Areas where the Board considers the Academy's practice strong.

Areas where the Board considers the Academy needs further work.

The Board's recommended priorities for the coming year.

Acknowledgment of any conflicts of interest, recusals, or governance issues encountered during the year.

### 18.3 Tone and framing

The Annual Report is honest and constructive. It does not exaggerate achievements; it does not minimize gaps. It is written in the same modest, accurate framing this Terms of Reference document promotes.

### 18.4 Distribution

The Annual Report is delivered to the Director.

The Director shares the report with Academy staff at the Director's discretion.

Where the Academy publishes a public Annual Report (Academy-level), an extract or summary of the Board's report may be included with Board approval. The published extract uses Board-approved language and does not misrepresent Board positions.

### 18.5 Signature

The Annual Report is signed by the Chair (or, where there is no Chair, by all Board members or by the senior member designated for the purpose).

## 19. Founding Recruitment Principles

The Academy is establishing the Board for the first time. Founding recruitment is a one-time act with long-lasting consequences. The principles below guide it.

### 19.1 Quality over speed

Recruiting the right members slowly is preferred to filling positions quickly with members of variable suitability. The first Board sets the institutional culture; later Boards inherit it. The Director shall resist pressure (internal or external) to populate the Board faster than substantive recruitment permits.

### 19.2 Substantive engagement before appointment

Prospective members are engaged in substantive conversation about the Academy's work before appointment. The conversation includes:

Honest description of the Academy's current state, including limitations.

Sharing of governance documents (this Terms of Reference, the Question Bank Governance Rule, the Research Methodology, the Privacy Review Checklist).

Discussion of the prospective member's expertise and how it would contribute.

Discussion of the time commitment, compensation arrangements, and confidentiality requirements.

Honest framing: this is a working advisory role for an institute under development, not a ceremonial post.

### 19.3 Avoidance of marketing-driven recruitment

Members are recruited for substantive contribution, not for the prestige their names lend the Academy. A prospective member who is willing to lend a name but unwilling to engage substantively is declined. This is honored in spirit as well as form: the Academy does not over-publicize Board membership for marketing purposes.

### 19.4 Honest disclosure of the Academy's standing

Prospective members are told accurately about:

The Academy's current institutional standing (specialized institute; not state-recognized higher education institution; not formally accredited).

The Academy's aspirations and the work toward them.

The Academy's existing publications and the basis on which they have been produced.

Known limitations and areas of active development.

Members who join under accurate disclosure are members the Academy can rely on. Members who join under inflated disclosure are members the Academy cannot rely on, and recruitment under such terms damages both parties.

### 19.5 Letter of appointment

Each appointed member receives a letter of appointment from the Director, specifying:

The term of appointment.

The expected time commitment.

Compensation arrangements (if any).

The member's confidentiality undertaking.

Reference to this Terms of Reference document.

Director's signature and member's countersignature.

## 20. Induction Process for New Members

New members, whether at founding or subsequently, undergo an induction process supporting their effective contribution from the outset.

### 20.1 Induction package

Each new member receives an induction package containing:

This Terms of Reference document (WPA-GOV-AAB-001).

The current Question Bank Governance Rule (WPA-GOV-QB-001).

The current Research Methodology (WPA-METH-001).

The current Privacy Review Checklist (WPA-PRIV-SD-001).

A summary of the Academy's recent publications, programs, and operational state.

Minutes of the previous year's Board meetings (where applicable).

The most recent Annual Advisory Summary Report (where applicable).

Confidentiality undertaking template for completion.

Conflict of interest declaration template for completion.

### 20.2 Induction meeting

Within four weeks of appointment, the Director holds an induction meeting with the new member. The meeting addresses:

Walkthrough of the Academy's structure, current activities, and key relationships.

Review of this Terms of Reference and clarification of any questions.

Discussion of the member's particular expertise and how it will be drawn upon.

Practical arrangements (communication channels, meeting calendar, support contacts).

### 20.3 First-year engagement

In the first year, the new member is supported through:

Pairing with an existing member where useful for orientation.

Lighter initial workload until the member has had opportunity to absorb the Academy's context.

Regular check-in with the Director to ensure the role is meeting expectations on both sides.

## 21. Resignation and Removal

### 21.1 Resignation

A member may resign at any time by written notice to the Director. The notice specifies the effective date, ideally allowing 60 days for transition. Resignation does not require justification, though members are encouraged to share reasons that may inform Board improvement.

### 21.2 Removal

The Director may remove a member from the Board for any of the following:

Material breach of confidentiality obligations.

Material breach of conflict of interest obligations.

Conduct inconsistent with the Board's role or the Academy's standards.

Persistent non-attendance or disengagement (typically defined as missing more than half of scheduled meetings in a 12-month period without acceptable reason).

Public statements misrepresenting the Board's positions or the Academy's standing in ways the member declines to correct after notice.

Loss of the qualifications that justified the member's appointment.

### 21.3 Removal procedure

Removal is communicated by the Director in writing, with reasons.

The member has the opportunity to respond in writing within 15 working days.

After consideration of the response, the Director confirms or rescinds the removal.

Removal records are retained.

### 21.4 Vacancy

A vacancy created by resignation or removal is filled following the recruitment principles of Section 19. A vacancy may remain open while suitable replacement is identified. The Board continues to function with reduced membership as long as quorum is achievable; if not, the Director gives priority to filling the vacancy.

### 21.5 Continuing obligations after departure

Departing members remain bound by confidentiality obligations and any other commitments that survive cessation of service per Section 14.3.

## 22. Limitation of Liability and Advisory-Only Status

### 22.1 Advisory-only status

This Terms of Reference establishes the Board as advisory only. Members do not exercise executive authority and do not bear executive responsibility for Academy decisions or operations. The Director and the Academy's executive function carry executive responsibility.

### 22.2 Liability

Members are not personally liable for the Academy's actions, contracts, debts, or omissions.

Members are not personally liable for advice given in good faith within the scope of their Board role, where the advice has been given honestly and on the basis of available information.

Members may be personally responsible for breach of their own duties (confidentiality breach, fraudulent statements, willful misconduct) — these remain personal responsibilities not limited by this section.

### 22.3 Indemnification

Where a Board member is named in a legal proceeding arising from their good-faith service on the Board (and not from breach of personal duties), the Academy shall, to the extent its resources permit and applicable law allows, support the member's defense, including legal costs reasonably incurred. Specific indemnification terms are recorded in the member's letter of appointment.

### 22.4 Insurance

The Academy may carry directors' and officers' liability insurance covering Board members. The existence and scope of any such insurance is communicated to Board members. The absence of insurance does not alter the advisory-only nature of the role and is not a matter members are expected to underwrite from personal resources.

### 22.5 No employment relationship

Service on the Board does not create an employment relationship between the member and the Academy. Members are independent advisors.

## 23. Honest Framing

### 23.1 What the Board is not

The Board is not, and shall not be represented as:

A formal accreditation body.

A body that confers university status, professional licensure, or external institutional recognition on the Academy.

A peer review committee in the scholarly sense.

A regulatory body.

An ethics committee with binding institutional authority.

A board of trustees, board of directors, or executive board.

### 23.2 What the Board is

The Board is an internal advisory function bringing independent academic, professional, and ethical perspectives to support the Academy's discipline and credibility. It is institution-building infrastructure operating within the limits this document establishes.

### 23.3 Public communication about the Board

Where the Academy describes the Board publicly:

The Board is described accurately as an “Academic Advisory Board” with advisory function.

Member names may be listed with their professional affiliations and titles, with each member's consent.

The Academy does not state or imply that Board membership confers external standing on the Academy.

The Academy does not use Board membership as a substitute for accreditation, recognition, or formal qualifications it does not have.

Members are not used as marketing instruments; their names are not deployed in promotional content beyond accurate Board listing.

### 23.4 Members' own framing

Members, in their own public communications, are encouraged to describe their Board service accurately:

“Member of the Academic Advisory Board, World Protocol Academy” is accurate.

Statements that imply the Board accredits, recognizes, or endorses the Academy beyond what is accurate are avoided.

### 23.5 The discipline this section serves

This section's framing protects all parties. It protects the Academy from credibility damage when claims are tested against reality. It protects Board members from being implicated in claims they did not endorse. It protects prospective students, candidates, and partners from misrepresentation about what the Academy and its certifications are. The discipline is internal but its effects are external.

## 24. Relationship with the Director

### 24.1 Director as principal interlocutor

The Director is the Board's principal interlocutor. Communications between the Board and the Academy flow primarily through the Director. The Director:

Convenes Board meetings and circulates agendas and materials.

Receives Board advice and decides on its incorporation.

Communicates Academy decisions back to the Board.

Bridges between the Board's advisory role and the Academy's operational decisions.

### 24.2 Director's accountability

The Director, while not subordinate to the Board, takes Board advice seriously. Where the Director declines Board advice on a substantive matter, the Director records the reasoning. Persistent disregard of substantive Board advice would itself be a matter the Board could raise in its Annual Advisory Summary Report.

### 24.3 Direct member communications

Individual Board members may communicate directly with each other and with relevant Academy staff for the conduct of their advisory work, with the Director kept informed. Members do not bypass the Director on substantive matters; routine working communication does not require formality.

### 24.4 Where the Director is conflicted

Where a Board matter implicates the Director's personal interests in a way that creates a substantive conflict (e.g., review of the Director's own publication, consideration of the Director's removal, allegation of misconduct against the Director), the Board may convene without the Director and provide advice through the Chair (or designated senior member). Such advice is recorded and provided in writing to the Director, except where matters require external escalation under applicable law.

### 24.5 Successor Director

In the event of change of Director (resignation, retirement, succession), the Board provides continuity. The Board's existing terms continue under the successor Director, who inherits the Director's role under this Terms of Reference. The Board may advise the Academy through any transition period.

## 25. Relationship with Future External Reviewers

### 25.1 The Board does not substitute for external review

The Board's review is internal review by qualified persons who are not Academy employees. It is not external peer review under the conventions of journals or scholarly publishers, and it is not external accreditation review. The distinctions are preserved deliberately.

### 25.2 External reviewers complementing the Board

As the Academy matures, it may engage external reviewers in addition to the Board, including:

Journal peer reviewers selected by editors of journals to which Academy work is submitted.

External monograph reviewers commissioned by the Academy or its publishers.

Independent auditors (academic, legal, technical) commissioned for specific governance audits per WPA-GOV-QB-001 §11.4.

Accreditation reviewers if and when the Academy seeks formal accreditation.

### 25.3 Coordination

Where external reviewers are engaged, the Board's role coordinates with theirs:

The Board does not duplicate work that external reviewers will perform; for example, where a manuscript will undergo peer review at a journal, the Board's review focuses on Academy-internal questions (alignment with WPA methodology, overclaiming, ethical considerations) rather than the substantive peer review the journal will conduct.

Where external review identifies issues, the Board may consider whether governance changes are warranted.

The Academy does not represent Board review and external review as interchangeable.

### 25.4 Toward formal external recognition

The Board's existence and operation may, over time, contribute to the Academy's case for formal external recognition (accreditation, mutual recognition, partnership status). The Board does not deliver such recognition, but the discipline of operating with a substantive Advisory Board demonstrates institutional seriousness that recognition processes typically value.

This is a long-term outcome, not a short-term claim. The Academy does not represent the Board's existence as evidence of recognition that has not been earned.
