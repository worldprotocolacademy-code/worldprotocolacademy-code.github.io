Ethics & AI Use

How WPA Journal handles research ethics and the responsible use of AI tools.

Human editorial responsibility

WPA Journal is edited by people. Human editorial responsibility for accuracy, integrity and final decisions is always required and cannot be delegated to automated tools.

Use of AI tools

AI tools may assist with language, structure or research support. They are aids, not authors. Authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy of all content they submit.

Disclosure of AI use

Where AI tools have meaningfully assisted the preparation of a manuscript, this should be disclosed clearly so readers and editors understand how the work was produced.

Citation integrity

Fabricated citations, unverified bibliographic data and false attribution of sources are not allowed.

Plagiarism

Plagiarism in any form is not accepted. All sources must be properly attributed and verifiable.

Conflicts of interest

Authors should disclose any financial, institutional or personal interests that could be perceived to influence the work.

WPA Journal is an independent institutional publication platform. External indexing, ISSN, DOI and other bibliographic statuses are listed only when officially obtained.
