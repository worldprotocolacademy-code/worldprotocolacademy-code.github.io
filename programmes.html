WPA Programme Doctrine

A ten-layer model for serious programme design.

WPA programmes are not random course lists. They are built on a defined doctrine covering identity, audience, outcomes, modules, exercises, testing, scenarios, certification, delivery and operational logic.

01

Programme Identity

Every programme must have a clear title, level, track, audience, purpose and outcome. No vague naming, no diluted positioning.

02

Audience Logic

Each programme is built for a defined public: institutions, protocol staff, PR roles, executive teams, students, serious learners or future trainers.

03

Learning Outcomes

Every pathway must produce knowledge, application, judgement and professional conduct — not passive attendance.

04

Module Architecture

Each programme is divided into clearly structured modules with core themes, subtopics and practical progression.

05

Lesson Design

Every module includes core lesson, applied explanation, case or scenario, practice task and mini assessment.

06

Exercise Logic

WPA uses knowledge drills, scenario exercises, protocol correction tasks and communication-response exercises.

07

Testing Structure

Programme testing is structured by module quizzes, mid-tests, final tests and practical assessment where needed.

08

Scenario Depth

Scenario work grows by level: basic scenarios, applied scenarios, full institutional scenarios and trainer-level teaching analysis.

09

Certification Logic

Certificates are tied to completion, score thresholds, scenario performance, progression and future verification standards.

10

Delivery and Operational Logic

Every programme is designed for self-paced, live online, in-person and institutional delivery — without losing structure.

Module Design

Every WPA module follows the same professional logic.

WPA modules are designed as repeatable units of serious learning: concept, application, case, exercise and assessment.

1. Core Lesson

The main doctrinal lesson, concept or professional standard.

2. Applied Explanation

How the rule or principle works in real professional settings.

3. Case or Scenario

A structured professional situation used for reading and judgement.

4. Practice Task

A concrete exercise: correction, response, briefing, speaking or protocol logic.

5. Mini Assessment

A short quiz or evaluated task that checks real comprehension and readiness.

Assessment and Testing

Assessment is built into the programme architecture.

WPA does not treat certification as attendance. Each level is tied to a defined assessment model, scenario logic and score threshold.

Foundation Assessment

  • 8 module quizzes × 15 questions
  • 1 mid-test × 30 questions
  • 1 final test × 50 questions
  • 1 short scenario
  • Focus: correctness, conduct, basic readiness

Professional Assessment

  • 10 module quizzes × 20 questions
  • 1 mid-test × 40 questions
  • 1 final test × 60 questions
  • 2 applied scenarios
  • 1 practical correction task

Advanced Assessment

  • 10 module quizzes × 20–25 questions
  • 1 mid-test × 50 questions
  • 1 final test × 80 questions
  • 3 institutional scenarios
  • 1 briefing or memo task

Trainer Assessment

  • 8 module quizzes × 15 questions
  • 1 final theory test × 50 questions
  • 2 teaching scenarios
  • 1 micro-teaching delivery
  • 1 assessment design task

Threshold logic

Recommended pass line: 70%. Distinction: 85%+. High distinction: 92%+. Certification should be linked to score, completion, scenario performance and future serial verification standards.

Standard WPA Training Day

How one live working day is structured.

WPA live delivery is designed to be disciplined, practical and cognitively clean: concept, application, case, exercise and debrief.

09:00–09:20
Opening and review — learning focus, objectives, recap of prior session and professional framing.
09:20–10:20
Core lesson — doctrine, rules, concepts and structured explanation.
10:20–10:35
Short break — reset and preparation for applied work.
10:35–11:20
Applied explanation — demonstrations, worked examples and practical interpretation.
11:20–12:10
Case or scenario work — guided scenario reading, professional diagnosis and response logic.
12:10–12:40
Exercise or correction task — seating logic, wording correction, protocol response, briefing or communication task.
12:40–13:00
Quiz, reflection and debrief — assessment, synthesis and next-step instruction.
