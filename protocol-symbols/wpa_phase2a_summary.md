## 3. Notable Findings

**United Kingdom (partial_consensus).** No single Act of Parliament establishes the Union Jack as the national flag — it holds status by precedent following the Acts of Union 1800. Documented; Wikipedia / Union Jack reference this same gap. WPA accepts 1 January 1801 (Acts of Union effective date) as canonical.

**Canada.** Two canonical dates exist: 28 January 1965 (royal proclamation signature) and 15 February 1965 (effective date / first raising on Parliament Hill). WPA accepts 15 February 1965 — consistent with the Government of Canada's National Flag of Canada Day. *Marked fully_verified because the Canadian state itself definitively recognises 15 February as the canonical date.*

**Australia.** Coat of Arms = current version 19 September 1912 (King George V Royal Warrant); earlier 7 May 1908 version preserved as `adoption_date_first`. Flag = 14 February 1954 (Flags Act assent), with December 1908 (seven-pointed Commonwealth Star gazetting) preserved as `adoption_date_first`.

**Netherlands.** Capital field carries an explicit `notes` clarifying the constitutional distinction: **Amsterdam = capital, The Hague = seat of government.** This is a protocol-critical distinction that other databases routinely confuse.

**Belgium.** Constitutional proportion **13:15** (unique among UN members), with civil ensign 2:3 also accepted. Trilingual official anthem (French/Dutch/German) properly documented in `official_name_local`.

**France.** No de jure coat of arms — the unofficial emblem (Robert Louis, 1953) is correctly classified as `national_emblem_non_heraldic`, not as a coat of arms. WPA does not invent a coat of arms where the state has not adopted one.

**Italy.** Italian national anthem was *de facto* from 1946 but *de jure* only from **4 December 2017** (Law No. 181/2017). Many sources still report 1946 — WPA uses the legally correct 2017 date.
